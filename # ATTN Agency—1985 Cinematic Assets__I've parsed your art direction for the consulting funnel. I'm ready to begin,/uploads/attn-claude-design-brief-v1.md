## 3. Aesthetic direction — "1985 one-sheet"

The register is a **vintage printed movie poster**, not a digital sci-fi UI.

**Yes:** airbrushed chrome and warm metallics · film grain and halftone screening · aged paper, tape, polaroids, receipts · matte-painting storm skies (desaturated blues `#1b2740` range) · warm rim light from below (fire/orange) · ink outlines and hard offset shadows · practical-effects realism.

**No:** neon glow UI, cyber grids, lens flares, glossy 3D renders, saturated cyan lighting, cartoon vector styling, anything that reads "digital."

**Litmus test for every asset:** could this exist as printed 1985 key art or a physical prop? If not, regrade it.

## 4. Licensing constraint (hard rule)

Homage, never reproduction. **No licensed Universal/BTTF assets**: no official logo artwork, no DeLorean branding or accurate DMC-12 likeness, no flux capacitor prop reproduction, no film stills.

- Hero vehicle = an *anonymous retro-futuristic sports coupe*, rear three-quarter, gullwing silhouette permitted, styling kept generic.
- "OUTATIME" plate is a direct prop reference — **decision sits with Will**; default to a neutral plate (`ATTN-88` suggested) unless he approves otherwise.
- Chrome type lockup stays as our own Anton-based build, not the film logo.

## 6. Colour grade notes

- Blacks warm, never pure: base `#0b0d12`, shadows lift toward `#100c08`.
- Storm blues desaturated 30–40%; no electric cyan in skies.
- Orange highlights bloom softly (practical light), never hard neon glow.
- Global: film grain, gentle vignette, slight halation on light sources.
- Contrast curve: filmic S-curve, lifted blacks acceptable — "aged print," not HDR.
